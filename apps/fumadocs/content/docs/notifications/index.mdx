GitPal notifications are meant to be useful, not noisy. You decide which events matter, where they go, and how urgent they should feel.

![GitPal notification center](/screenshots/notifications-center.svg)

## Channels

- Resend for email.
- Slack for team chat.
- Microsoft Teams for workspace chat.
- Telegram for quick personal alerts.
- Linear for issue-tracking follow-up.

## What gets sent

- Review outcomes.
- Billing events.
- AI workflow status.
- Webhook failures and retries.
- Correctness, security, performance, maintainability, testing, documentation, and architecture signals.

## How to tune the noise

- Start with success, warning, and error severity filters.
- Turn off categories you already track elsewhere.
- Keep review and webhook alerts on by default.
- Use the channel test button before you depend on a new destination.

## What to expect

- Every channel stores its target preview so you can confirm where messages will go.
- Delivery failures are recorded and reflected in observability.
- Channel credentials stay encrypted at rest.

## Good defaults

- Use Slack or Teams for shared team alerts.
- Use Resend for one-person email updates.
- Use Telegram or Linear for high-signal personal follow-up.
- Keep billing alerts enabled even when everything else is quiet.
